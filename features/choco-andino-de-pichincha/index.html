Why it is important

Biodiversity + conservation

The Chocó Andino de Pichincha Biosphere Reserve was nominated in 2018 and spans 286,806 hectares. [oai_citation:0‡UNESCO](https://www.unesco.org/en/mab/choco-andino-de-pichincha) It stretches from low foothill forests to high Andean landscapes (roughly 360m to 4,480m), creating a huge range of habitats in one region. [oai_citation:1‡UNESCO](https://www.unesco.org/en/mab/choco-andino-de-pichincha)

That habitat diversity is why the area is recognized for biodiversity and endemism. UNESCO notes thousands of plant species and rich wildlife across mammals, reptiles, birds, and amphibians. [oai_citation:2‡UNESCO](https://www.unesco.org/en/mab/choco-andino-de-pichincha)

How to experience it

Simple guest guide

Think of Chocó Andino as a giant outdoor living room: every road turn can reveal a new microclimate, a new set of birds, and a new trail. The best experiences here are slow and sensory.

  • Start early for birds and soft light.
  • Plan for short hikes with waterfall breaks.
  • Bring a light rain layer. Cloud forest weather shifts fast.
  • Support local guides and community projects - it is part of what makes a biosphere reserve work.
