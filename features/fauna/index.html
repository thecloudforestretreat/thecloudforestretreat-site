Fauna

From colorful birds to elusive mammals, the cloud forest surrounding The Cloud Forest Retreat is alive with movement, sound, and rare encounters.

The Chocó Andino region is one of the richest wildlife habitats in the world. Birds dominate the skies and canopy, while mammals like the spectacled bear quietly roam forest corridors below.

Birdlife of the The Cloud Forest Retreat

Toucans

Ramphastidae family
Iconic Fruit-eaters
Known for their oversized bills and vibrant colors, toucans play a key role in seed dispersal throughout the forest.

Hummingbirds

Trochilidae family
Pollinators Fast flyers
Tiny and tireless, hummingbirds feed on nectar and help pollinate countless flowering plants across the forest.

Spectacled Bear

Tremarctos ornatus
Endangered Keystone species
South America’s only bear species, the spectacled bear is rarely seen but plays a vital role in maintaining forest balance.
